  • What is Lifehelp about?

    The mission of Lifehelp is to make therapy accessible and affordable for everyone. We aim to provide more people with a convenient way to contact licensed therapists who can help those in need to make their life happier.

    This service was created not to replace in-office therapy, but we want more people to benefit from therapy and overcome their day-to-day challenges in a stigma-free environment.

    When using Lifehelp, you can send a Counselor text, audio, picture, and video messages in a private chat room. Lifehelp stands out because of the affordability and availability it provides.

    Lifehelp is the largest online counseling platform worldwide. We change the way people get help in dealing with their issues by making professional counseling available anytime and anywhere. Lifehelp provides convenient, safe, and affordable access to services of licensed therapists using a computer, tablet, or smartphone.

  • How can Lifehelp improve my life?

    On Lifehelp you can find thousands of licensed therapists, many of whom are trained in multiple areas of expertise. We help people with the following and more:

    • Couple & pre-marriage problems
    • Marriage & family problems
    • Children & parenthood problems
    • Parents & other family relations problems
    • Mental health problems
    • Sex & intimacy problems
    • Anxiety
    • Addiction
    • Depression
    • Eating Disorders
    • PTSD (Post Traumatic Stress Disorder)
    • LGBT Issues

  • Is Lifehelp right for me?

    Lifehelp is right for everyone who wants to improve the quality of their life. We can help you if there is anything that interferes with your happiness or prevents you from achieving your goals or does not give you peace of mind. Besides, we have counselors who are specialized in specific issues, such as stress, depression, anxiety, self-esteem, parenting, relationships, addictions, eating disorders, family conflicts, trauma, abuse, anger, LGBT, grief, and more.

    Lifehelp is not the right solution for you if the following statements are true for you and your situation:

    • You have thoughts of hurting yourself or others
    • You are a minor or you are under the care of a legal guardian
    • You are in an urgent crisis or an emergency situation
    • You have been diagnosed with a severe mental illness, or if you have been advised to be in psychological supervision or psychiatric care
    • You were required to undergo therapy or counseling either by a court order or by any other authority
    • You do not have a device that can connect to the Internet or you do not have a reliable Internet connection.

  • Can Lifehelp substitute for traditional face-to-face therapy?

    All the professionals who provide services through Lifehelp are licensed and credentialed therapists and were certified by their state's board to provide therapy and counseling. Nevertheless, while the service may have similar benefits, it's not capable of replacing traditional face-to-face therapy in every case. Please turn your attention to the fact that your Counselor won't be able to make any official diagnosis, to fulfill any court order or prescribe medication.

  • Who will be helping me?

    Since you're signed up, we will match you to an available Counselor who fits your preferences, goals, and the type of issues you are dealing with. Counselors have different approaches, methods, and areas of focus. That is why it's essential to find the right person to achieve the best results for you. We want to note that our service is able to provide a successful match most of the time. However, you may elect to be matched to a different counselor if you think that your Counselor isn't a good fit for you.

  • Who are the counselors?

    All the Counselors on Lifehelp are licensed, trained, experienced, and accredited psychologists (Ph.D. / PsyD), clinical social workers (LCSW / LMSW), marriage and family therapists (LMFT), or licensed professional counselors (LPC). They all have a Masters Degree or a Doctorate Degree in their field. They have been qualified and certified by their state's professional board after successfully completing the necessary education, exams, training, and practice. Their experience, expertise, and background may vary, but they all possess at least 3 years and 2,000 hours of hands-on experience.

  • Are Lifehelp therapists licensed?

    Certainly! On Lifehelp we have a huge, nationwide network of licensed therapists. All the therapists have been carefully checked and verified to provide services on Lifehelp. On our platform there are no interns or therapists who are not fully licensed and still under supervision. We require our therapists to have over 3,000 hours of clinical experience.

    Most of the Lifehelp therapists have additional training in specific approaches to therapy, which include Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, Existential-Humanistic, Dialectical Behavioral Therapy, Psychodynamic, Mindfulness, and more.

    Please note that the therapists you work with are licensed in your US state of residence.

    Besides, we also have a Board of Advisors who include prominent professionals that are experts in the healthcare space and help ensure that our service is useful and effective in achieving our goals.

  • How are the counselors verified?

    With the help of our team, we make sure that every provider we bring to the platform is fully licensed and in good standing. Mandatory requirement for therapists who apply is to provide proper licensure documentation, proof of identity, and references from other licensed practitioners who have worked with them. We always cross-check their licensure information with their respective state licensing board.

    Besides, each potential provider needs to complete a case study exam by a licensed clinician and be evaluated in a video interview. After this rigorous 4-5 week process, only about 15% of the therapists who apply to work through Lifehelp are accepted to the platform.

    On Lifehelp, you'll find the full licensing information for each provider so that you could do your own due diligence on your Counselor.

    There are three ways you can get counseling services:

    • Chatting with your counselor.
    • Speaking over the phone with your counselor.
    • Video conferencing with your counselor.

    You can choose different ways at different times according to your requirements, availability, and convenience.

  • How long can I use Lifehelp?

    There is no time frame. Everything depends on your needs and may vary from one person to another. Some people only need a few weeks to get the benefit and significant result from the consultations. The others prefer to stick to the program for an extended period of time. Deciding on the number of consultations and time is entirely up to you.

  • Is online therapy really effective?

    Definitely yes. Over the last decade, there have been presented numerous research studies that show the effective potential of online therapeutic conversation. Here's a good start for showing the breadth of the telemedicine and online medicine field:

    • Distance Therapy Comes of Age. Scientific American.
    • Internet-based versus face-to-face cognitive-behavioral intervention for depression: A randomized controlled non-inferiority trial.
    • Outcomes of 98,609 U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs Patients Enrolled in Telemental Health Services.
    • Internet-based and other computerized psychological treatments for adult depression: A meta-analysis. Cognitive Behaviour Therapy.
    • A comprehensive review and a meta-analysis of the effectiveness of internet-based psychotherapeutic interventions.
    • The effectiveness of Web-based vs. non-Web-based interventions: a meta-analysis of behavioral change outcomes. Journal of Medical Internet Research.
    • Internet-versus group-administered cognitive behavior therapy for panic disorder in a psychiatric setting: a randomized trial.
    • Home telemental health implementation and outcomes using electronic messaging.

  • How do you protect my privacy?

    Your privacy and safety are always a high priority for us. We go to great lengths to ensure that you and your data are always kept secure and confidential. We can assure you that our technology is fully HIPAA compliant, and your safety is our #1 priority.

    All of your chat data is encrypted on the servers. All communication between our software and the servers is encrypted. On Lifehelp app we require you to enter your password and allow you to create a unique passcode for extra security. Furthermore, our iOS app uses Touch ID and Face ID on enabled devices.

    Do you have questions about privacy and security? Don't hesitate to write to support@lifehelp.co.

  • When is Lifehelp available?

    Lifehelp is available around the clock. 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and 365 days a year, even on holidays. You can use Lifehelp anytime when you have a non-emergency medical condition or if you are unable to see your primary care provider. You can also use it when you simply prefer a more convenient, cost-effective alternative to the emergency room, urgent care center, or clinic.

  • What is the refund policy?

    Lifehelp seeks to ensure everyone the best possible experience with our platform and Counselors. At any time, you can request a new Counselor if you consider that this one doesn't fit you or you're not satisfied with their work. You can do it through your account settings.

    Lifehelp acts as a conduit to arrange professional services. Those services are paid to the therapists through their Therapist Agreement with Lifehelp. In general, we do not provide refunds for unused time. Just like other medical professionals, the therapists charge for the time that is scheduled to be used. However, customer service reviews refunds on a case-by-case basis after considering the client’s requests and the therapist’s response. We can work out a compromise in case of unique or legal circumstances (medical, death, disability, court orders, etc.) that may dictate the reason for the refund request. In cases where we cannot provide a refund, we are usually able to make accommodations some other way such as a credit for future service.

    If there is something you believe we could do differently or you have any suggestions on how we can improve your Lifehelp experience, please let us know by contacting us at support@lifehelp.co.

  • After I start the therapy, will I have the same therapist?

    Surely! Your Counselor will personally work with you throughout all the days and weeks you are at Lifehelp to help you get the best results.

  • Can I stay anonymous?

    After signing up on Lifehelp, we do not ask you for your full name or contact information. You can pick any nickname you prefer to identify you in the system. When you start the counseling process, we will ask you for emergency contact information. We do this for your safety if your Counselor believes that you or someone else might be in danger. You can be sure that all the information is kept safe in our system.

    Do you have questions about privacy and security? Email us at support@lifehelp.co.

  • How do I cancel my account?

    You can cancel your account at any time. To do this, please reach out to support@lifehelp.co We will provide you with detailed instructions and assistance.
